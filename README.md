## Key Findings

| Finding | Implication for R&I |
|---|---|
| Ingredients + highlights explain only 13% of rating variance | Brand positioning and price drive consumer satisfaction more than formulation alone |
| `Clean at Sephora` certification is the strongest formulation-adjacent signal | Certification programs outweigh individual ingredient claims in consumer perception |
| Hair Dye & Color Care subcategories rate **below** portfolio average (3.84–3.98 vs 4.22) | Direct formulation opportunity — color products underperform vs treatment formats |
| Tea tree (+0.184) and shea butter (+0.066) show positive rating delta | Underutilized ingredients with above-average consumer satisfaction |
| Silicone (-0.122) and caffeine (-0.157) show negative rating delta | Despite heavy marketing, these ingredients correlate with lower ratings |
| Dry Shampoo is the lowest-rated subcategory (3.88) | Clear whitespace for reformulation investment |
| `Good for: Color Care` appears in 200 products, averaging 4.31 | Color-protective positioning is at parity with portfolio — room to differentiate |
